Important Reminder

You have 5 minutes maximum to convince the "boss". Be concise, clear and enthusiastic!

You must present without written notes but you can show your flyer and images.

1. Introduction

Duration: 30 seconds

Hook

"Hello [Boss's name], today I'm not presenting you with a simple menu, but a real journey. Get ready to take off for Japan with McDonald's new menu: the Sakura Edition!"

Menu Presentation

Show your flyer and quickly present the 3 products:

  • McWagyu Burger
  • Nori Sesame Fries
  • Matcha Milkshake

Tip: Start strong with an enthusiastic tone. Look your "boss" in the eyes.

2. Argument 1: Following the trend

Duration: 1 minute

Key Points

  • Japan is extremely popular among young people
  • Limited edition = sense of urgency
  • Viral potential on social media

What you can say

"Japan is extremely popular right now, especially among young people with manga, anime and all Japanese culture. By launching our 'Sakura Edition' menu, we directly target this clientele who is ready to spend to live a Japanese experience. It's a limited edition, so everyone will rush to try it before it disappears. And of course, it's going to be a hit on TikTok and Instagram!"

[Image: Show examples of Japan's popularity - manga, cherry blossoms, etc.]

3. Argument 2: Premium image and profit

Duration: 1 minute 30 seconds

Key Points

  • Wagyu beef = luxury product
  • Higher price = higher margin
  • Brand image improvement

What you can say

"We use Wagyu beef, which is recognized as one of the best in the world. It's a luxury product that shows McDonald's can also offer premium products. Customers are willing to pay more for something exceptional, which allows us to increase our margin on each sale. This menu improves our brand image by showing that McDonald's can also be synonymous with quality and innovation."

[Image: Photo of the McWagyu Burger highlighting its premium aspect]

4. Argument 3: Unique customer experience

Duration: 1 minute 30 seconds

Key Points

  • Complete experience, not just a meal
  • Consistent menu from start to finish
  • Increased average basket

What you can say

"We're not just selling a meal, we're selling a complete experience. The nori and sesame fries, the matcha milkshake... everything is designed for total immersion in Japanese culture. Customers won't just come for a burger, but to experience a trip to Japan during a meal. This consistency will encourage them to take the complete menu rather than separate products, which automatically increases our average basket."

[Images: Photos of fries and milkshake that complete the experience]

5. Conclusion

Duration: 30 seconds

Summary

"To summarize, the Sakura Edition menu has three major advantages: more young customers attracted by the Japanese trend, more profits thanks to premium products, and a better brand image with a unique customer experience. It's a brilliant idea that will get people talking about us and boost our sales."

Call to action

"So, when do we launch the Sakura Edition?"

Tip: End with confidence and enthusiasm. Leave a moment of silence after your final question for more impact.

Checklist before presentation

  • Flyer printed or ready to be shown
  • Product images prepared
  • Arguments memorized (no need word for word)
  • Timing rehearsed to fit in 5 minutes
  • Professional attire
